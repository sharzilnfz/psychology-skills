# Life Psychology — Frameworks Reference

Detailed framework application for the life-psychology skill.
Loaded on demand during specific modules.

## Logotherapy (Viktor Frankl)

### Core Principle

"He who has a WHY to live can bear almost any HOW."

Meaning is not invented — it is discovered through:

1. **Creative values** — what you give to the world (work, art, contribution)
2. **Experiential values** — what you receive from the world (love, beauty, nature)
3. **Attitudinal values** — the stance you take toward unavoidable suffering

### Application in Module 30

- "Even in this difficult situation, what feels meaningful to you?"
- "If this struggle had something to teach you, what might it be?"
- "What would you do with your time if money and status didn't matter?"

### Existential Vacuum Indicators

Signs the user may be experiencing meaninglessness:
- Chronic boredom despite having options
- Sunday neurosis (anxiety during unstructured time)
- Compensatory drives (power, pleasure, busyness as meaning substitutes)
- "What's the point?" as a recurring feeling, not just a rhetorical question

## Narrative Therapy (White & Epston) — apply in Module 20

### Externalization

Separate the person from the problem:
- Not "I am anxious" → "Anxiety shows up when..."
- Not "I'm stuck" → "This stuckness has been visiting — when did it first arrive?"
- Not "I'm a failure" → "There's a failure story operating here — whose voice is it in?"

### Unique Outcomes

Moments that contradict the dominant narrative:
- "Has there ever been a time when [the problem] was present but you responded differently?"
- "What does that exception tell you about yourself?"
- "If that moment were a seed, what kind of life could grow from it?"

### Re-Authoring Questions

- "What title would you give the next chapter?"
- "Who would be a character in the story you *want* to live?"
- "What would [person you admire] notice about you that you're not seeing?"

### Definitional Ceremony (simplified)

For the 90_SYNTHESIS:
1. User tells their re-authored story
2. Agent reflects what resonated (specific images, phrases, values)
3. Agent names what the story reveals about the user's identity
4. Agent asks: "What becomes possible if this is the true story?"

## PERMA Model (Seligman) — apply in Module 30

### Five Pillars of Flourishing

| Pillar | Definition | Audit Question |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **P** — Positive Emotion | Joy, gratitude, serenity, interest, hope | "When did you last feel genuinely good? What were you doing?" |
| **E** — Engagement | Flow, absorption, using signature strengths | "What activities make time disappear for you?" |
| **R** — Relationships | Love, intimacy, social connection | "Who do you feel most yourself around?" |
| **M** — Meaning | Belonging to something beyond self | "What contribution matters to you — even if no one noticed?" |
| **A** — Achievement | Mastery, accomplishment, winning | "What have you done recently that you're genuinely proud of?" |

### Scoring Guide

For each pillar, explore:
1. Current state (1-10)
2. What a 10 would look like (concrete)
3. What one small shift would raise the score by 1
4. Which pillars are carrying the others (compensatory patterns)

## Bridges' Transition Model — apply in Module 40

### Three Phases

```
[1. ENDINGS]  →  [2. NEUTRAL ZONE]  →  [3. NEW BEGINNINGS]
 Letting go       In-between space       Fresh start
 Grief/loss       Confusion/creativity   Energy/purpose
 Identity shift   Reorientation          New identity
```

### Phase Identification Questions

**Endings:**
- "What are you losing or leaving behind — even if you chose to leave?"
- "What hasn't been properly grieved or acknowledged?"
- "What parts of your old identity are you holding onto?"

**Neutral Zone:**
- "What feels uncertain or up in the air right now?"
- "Are you trying to rush through this phase?"
- "What is this in-between time making possible that 'before' didn't?"

**New Beginnings:**
- "What's starting to feel exciting or possible?"
- "What small signs of the new direction have appeared?"
- "What would the first concrete step look like?"

### Critical Insight

People want to jump from Endings → New Beginnings without sitting in the
Neutral Zone. The Neutral Zone is where transformation happens. If the
user is in it, normalize it — do not try to speed it up.

## Erikson's Psychosocial Stages (apply contextually)

### Relevant Adult Stages

| Stage | Crisis | Age Range | Core Question |
|-------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Intimacy vs. Isolation | Young adult | ~20-40 | "Can I love and be loved?" |
| Generativity vs. Stagnation | Middle adult | ~40-65 | "Am I contributing something that outlasts me?" |
| Integrity vs. Despair | Late adult | 65+ | "Has my life been meaningful?" |

### Application

Use to normalize the user's struggle as developmentally appropriate:
- "This question about whether your work matters — that's not a random
  crisis. It's the central question of this life stage."
- Name the developmental task without pathologizing it
