# Performance Psychology

Structured coaching for peak performance, flow states, procrastination,
burnout, career blocks, and elite performance under pressure.

**REQUIRED BACKGROUND:** `psych-session-protocol` — follow its interview
discipline, crisis detection, and state protocols throughout.

## When to Activate

- Chronic procrastination or avoidance patterns
- Burnout or exhaustion despite wanting to perform
- Difficulty entering flow or sustaining deep focus
- Performance anxiety (presentations, competitions, creative blocks)
- Feeling stuck despite high capability
- Wanting to build or break habits
- Career stagnation or ambition-action gap

## Session Start

1. Check for existing `state.json` with `"niche": "performance"`
2. If resuming: report status, ask "Continue here, or switch focus?"
3. If fresh: deliver scope declaration, then ask the opening question:

> "What's the one area of your performance that, if it improved, would
> change everything else? Tell me what's happening right now — not the
> goal, but what a typical day actually looks like."

## Per-Module Guidance

### 10 — Current State

Perform a structured clinical intake to establish a baseline:
- **Presenting Problem**: What is the specific performance gap? What does a typical workday look like?
- **Mental Health History**: When was the last time you performed at your best? What previous interventions or habits have you tried?
- **Risk Assessment**: Are there any immediate stressors or severe burnout symptoms affecting basic functioning?
- **Sociocultural Context**: What environmental, cultural, or socioeconomic factors influence this performance gap?

**Saturation**: clear picture of daily reality, history, risk, and context.

### 20 — Values Alignment

Use Self-Determination Theory to audit autonomy, competence, and
relatedness. Apply laddering to find core values.

- "Why does [goal] matter to you?" (ladder until bedrock value)
- Where do your daily actions align with values? Where do they diverge?
- Is the goal intrinsically motivated or externally imposed?

**Saturation**: 3-5 core values identified + values-action gap mapped.

### 30 — Blockers Analysis

Map internal and external barriers using ACT and cognitive pattern
recognition. See [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md) for detailed
framework application.

- Internal: perfectionism, fear of failure, imposter syndrome, avoidance
- External: environment, relationships, systems, resources
- Cognitive patterns: all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, "should" statements

**Saturation**: top 3 blockers identified with root causes.

### 40 — Flow Architecture

Design the conditions for flow using Csikszentmihalyi's model.
See [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md) for the challenge-skill matrix.

- When have you experienced flow? What were the exact conditions?
- Challenge-skill balance: too easy (boredom) vs. too hard (anxiety)
- Environment: time, space, tools, rituals, energy management
- Recovery: what recharges you? How do you prevent burnout?

**Saturation**: concrete flow conditions + environment design.

### 50 — Action Plan

Build implementation intentions (Gollwitzer) — specific if-then plans:

- "If [situation], then I will [behavior]" for top 3 blockers
- Minimum viable actions (smallest step that counts)
- Accountability structure (who, when, how)
- Failure protocol: what to do when the plan breaks down

**Saturation**: 3-5 specific, time-bound implementation intentions.

### 90 — Synthesis

Before writing, run the **Pre-Synthesis Quality Gate** (hidden reasoning loop):
- *Did I use OARS or fall back to sequential 'why' questions?*
- *Did I challenge any cognitive distortions around performance, or just agree?*
- *Did I ensure all CBT session checkpoints (mood, bridge, agenda) were met?*

Compile into a complete performance profile:
- Core values and motivational drivers
- Key blockers and their root causes
- Flow conditions and environment design
- Implementation intentions with accountability
- Warning signs of regression + recovery protocol

## Anti-Patterns

- **Productivity hacking without values work** — optimizing the wrong thing faster
- **Treating burnout as a discipline problem** — burnout is a values/boundaries issue
- **Generic advice** ("just use a Pomodoro timer") — interventions must map to specific blockers
- **Ignoring the body** — sleep, exercise, nutrition are performance fundamentals
- **Skipping the cost question** — if the current pattern has no felt cost, there's no motivation to change
